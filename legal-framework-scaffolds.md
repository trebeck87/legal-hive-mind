## Business / Corporate / Contracts

**Federal**: Securities regulation (Securities Act of 1933, Exchange Act of 1934, SEC rules). Bankruptcy (Title 11 USC). Antitrust (Sherman Act, Clayton Act, FTC Act). UCC governs commercial transactions (adopted by all states with variations).

**State variation traps**: Corporate formation and governance law is entirely state-specific — Delaware, Nevada, and Wyoming are common incorporation states with materially different rules. LLC operating agreement enforceability varies by state. Non-compete enforceability ranges from fully enforceable (most states) to banned (California, Minnesota, Oklahoma, North Dakota — VERIFY current list as this is actively changing). Statute of frauds requirements vary. Usury limits are state-specific. Choice-of-law and forum selection clause enforceability varies.

⚠ **HIGH VARIATION**: Non-compete law is in active flux — multiple states have changed rules in recent years. Always verify current status.

## Real Estate / Landlord-Tenant

**Federal**: Fair Housing Act (Title VIII). RESPA for mortgage transactions. ADA for commercial properties. No general federal landlord-tenant law — almost entirely state-governed.

**State variation traps**: Commercial vs. residential tenant protections vary enormously. Rent control exists in some cities/states but is banned in others. Eviction procedures, timelines, and notice requirements are state-specific. Security deposit limits and return deadlines differ by state. Implied warranty of habitability scope varies. Lease termination on property sale — default rule (lease survives) is near-universal, but specific tenant protections differ. Property tax and transfer tax structures are state/county-specific. Recording requirements for deeds and liens vary by state. Community property vs. common law property states affects ownership analysis.

⚠ **HIGH VARIATION**: Landlord-tenant law is the most state-specific area in practice. Never assume one state's rules apply in another.

## Employment / Labor

**Federal**: Title VII (discrimination), ADA, ADEA, FLSA (wage/hour), FMLA, NLRA (union/collective bargaining), OSHA, ERISA (benefits), WARN Act (mass layoffs — 100+ employees), COBRA, IRCA (immigration/I-9). Federal contractors: Executive Order 11246, Davis-Bacon, Service Contract Act.

**State variation traps**: At-will employment exceptions vary dramatically — some states recognize implied contract and good faith exceptions, others don't. State minimum wages often exceed federal. Meal and rest break requirements are state-specific (California is notably strict). Pay transparency and salary history bans are state-by-state. State WARN Acts often have lower employee thresholds than federal. State anti-discrimination laws often cover more protected classes than federal. Noncompete and nonsolicitation enforceability (see Business section). Workers' compensation is entirely state-run. State paid family/medical leave programs exist in growing number of states. Final paycheck timing requirements vary.

⚠ **HIGH VARIATION**: Wage/hour compliance is the #1 multi-state employment trap. California, New York, and Illinois have particularly complex state-specific regimes.

## IP / Technology / Data Privacy

**Federal**: Patent Act (35 USC), Copyright Act (17 USC), Lanham Act (trademarks, 15 USC §1051+), DMCA, CFAA (computer fraud), ECPA (electronic communications), CAN-SPAM, COPPA (children's privacy), HIPAA (health data), GLBA (financial data). Trade secrets: Defend Trade Secrets Act (federal) + state UTSA adoptions.

**State variation traps**: Data breach notification laws are state-specific — all 50 states have them, but triggers, timelines, and requirements differ. Comprehensive state privacy laws (California CCPA/CPRA, Virginia VCDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA, others — VERIFY current list as new states adopt regularly). Right of publicity is state-specific with wide variation. Anti-SLAPP statutes vary significantly. State trade secret law nuances exist even among UTSA adopters. AI regulation is emerging at state level with no uniformity.

⚠ **HIGH VARIATION**: Data privacy law is the fastest-moving area in US law. New state laws take effect regularly. Always verify current status.

## Regulatory / Compliance

**Federal**: Administrative Procedure Act (APA) governs federal rulemaking. Major agencies by sector: EPA (environment), FDA (food/drugs), FTC (consumer protection/competition), FCC (communications), SEC (securities), CFPB (consumer finance), DOT/FMCSA (transport), ATF (firearms/alcohol/tobacco), DEA (controlled substances), FERC (energy). Dodd-Frank (financial regulation). BSA/AML/KYC (anti-money laundering).

**State variation traps**: Cannabis — legal status varies by state, remains federally illegal (Schedule I). VERIFY current status per state as this changes frequently. Alcohol licensing is state-specific (three-tier system variations). State environmental regulations often exceed federal minimums (California is notably stricter). State consumer protection (UDAP) statutes vary in scope and private right of action availability. Insurance regulation is primarily state-based. Professional licensing requirements are state-specific. State-level agency enforcement varies significantly.

⚠ **HIGH VARIATION**: Cannabis law, alcohol regulation, and insurance are overwhelmingly state-governed despite federal overlay.

## Litigation / Disputes

**Federal**: Federal Rules of Civil Procedure, Federal Rules of Evidence, 28 USC (jurisdiction, venue). Class actions: Rule 23, CAFA. Arbitration: Federal Arbitration Act. Key doctrines: Erie (state law in federal court), diversity jurisdiction ($75K+), supplemental jurisdiction. Anti-SLAPP (no federal statute — state-specific).

**State variation traps**: Statutes of limitation vary by state AND by claim type — contract, tort, fraud, property all have different periods in different states. Discovery rules and scope differ. State court procedural rules are not identical to federal. Damages caps (especially medical malpractice) are state-specific. Joint and several liability rules vary. Comparative vs. contributory negligence — most states are comparative, but a few retain pure contributory negligence (VERIFY: Alabama, Maryland, North Carolina, Virginia, DC). Prejudgment interest rules differ. Arbitration enforceability has state-specific nuances despite FAA preemption.

⚠ **HIGH VARIATION**: Statutes of limitation are the most dangerous trap in multi-state litigation. Always verify per state per claim type.

## Tax / Financial Planning

**Federal**: Internal Revenue Code (Title 26 USC). Key structures: income tax, capital gains, estate/gift tax, FICA/payroll, excise. Entity taxation: C-corp, S-corp, partnership/LLC (pass-through), sole proprietor. Retirement: 401(k)/IRA governed by IRC + ERISA. Estate/gift: lifetime exemption, annual exclusion, generation-skipping transfer tax. International: FATCA, GILTI, FDII, BEAT, tax treaties.

**State variation traps**: State income tax — some states have none (Texas, Florida, Nevada, Washington, Wyoming, South Dakota, Alaska, New Hampshire — VERIFY current list). State corporate tax structures vary (franchise tax, gross receipts, corporate income). State estate/inheritance taxes exist in some states with lower exemptions than federal. State sales tax — rates and what's taxable vary enormously. Pass-through entity tax elections (SALT workaround) — availability and rules are state-specific. State nexus rules for business taxation. Property tax is county/municipality-level.

⚠ **HIGH VARIATION**: Multi-state tax exposure is the most commonly overlooked issue for businesses operating across state lines.

## Personal / Family

**Federal**: Limited federal role — PKPA (custody jurisdiction), ICWA (Indian child welfare), UCCJEA (adopted by states, not federal law per se), Hague Convention (international child abduction), federal bankruptcy interaction with state family law.

**State variation traps**: Family law is almost entirely state-governed. Divorce grounds, property division (community property vs. equitable distribution), alimony/spousal support calculations, child custody standards, and child support guidelines all vary by state. Adoption law is state-specific — some states allow private placement, others require agency involvement. Surrogacy enforceability ranges from fully enforceable to criminalized depending on state. Grandparent visitation rights vary. Domestic partnership/civil union recognition varies. Prenuptial agreement enforceability standards differ. Guardianship and conservatorship procedures are state-specific. Medicaid planning for elder law is state-specific (different income/asset thresholds).

⚠ **HIGH VARIATION**: Family law and elder law are the most state-specific personal law areas. Never extrapolate one state's rules to another.
